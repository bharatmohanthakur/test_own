### 1. Nemotron-Cascade 2 Paper Released (March 19, 2026) -- CRITICAL

**Paper**: arxiv 2603.19220
**Model**: Nemotron-Cascade-2-30B-A3B (same base architecture as our competition model!)
**Key Achievement**: IMO Gold (35/42), IOI Gold (439/600), ICPC Gold (10/12)

#### Full Training Recipe (7 Cascade RL Stages):
1. **Instruction-Following RL (IF-RL)** -- ~180 steps, LR=3e-6
2. **Multi-Domain RL** -- ~70 steps (tool calling, STEM, structured output)
3. **Multi-Domain On-Policy Distillation (MOPD)** -- 40-50 steps, LR=2e-6
4. **RLHF** -- human alignment
5. **Long-Context RL** -- reasoning over massive inputs
6. **Code RL** -- competitive coding, 3.5K filtered samples
7. **SWE RL** -- software engineering

#### GRPO Hyperparameters (from paper):
- **LR**: 3e-6
- **Batch size**: 128 prompts
- **Group size (G)**: 16 rollouts per prompt
- **Temperature**: 1.0, Top-p: 1.0
- **Optimizer**: AdamW
- **KL coeff**: 0 (removed entirely!)
- **Entropy coeff**: 0
- **Objective**: Simplified to standard REINFORCE with group-normalized rewards

#### Critical Insights:
- **KL term removed entirely** -- simplifies GRPO to REINFORCE with group-normalization
- **On-policy only** -- single gradient update per batch of rollouts
- **Binary rewards for math/code** -- strict pass/fail, no partial credit
- **Domain-specific RL stages resist catastrophic forgetting**
- **MOPD outperforms GRPO**: reaches 92.0 on AIME25 in 30 steps vs GRPO's 91.0 in 25 steps

#### Released Data (HuggingFace):
- **SFT Data**: nvidia/Nemotron-Cascade-2-SFT-Data (~25.6M samples across 10+ domains)
  - Math: 5.2M, Code: 4.2M, Science: 2.7M, Chat: 14M
  - SFT config: batch=64, packed_seq_len=256K, LR=5e-5, cosine scheduler, ~1.5 epochs
- **RL Data**: nvidia/Nemotron-Cascade-2-RL-data (~55K samples)
  - IF-RL: 45,879 samples, MOPD: 6,170 samples, SWE-RL: 3,611 samples

#### Actionable for Competition:
- Use Nemotron-Cascade-2 SFT data (math + science subsets) for broader reasoning training
- The GRPO recipe (LR=3e-6, group_size=16, no KL) is directly applicable to our LoRA setup
- MOPD is more sample-efficient than GRPO -- consider implementing

### 2. RO-GRPO: Routing-Optimized GRPO for MoE Models -- HIGHLY RELEVANT

**Paper**: OpenReview rhD7ZuFAjU
**Problem**: Standard GRPO causes routing collapse in MoE models (like Nemotron-3-Nano!)
**Solution**: RO-GRPO transforms routing statistics into mechanism-aware rewards

#### Key Innovation:
- Standard GRPO with LoRA on MoE leads to underutilization of expert adapters
- RO-GRPO adds routing balance reward to the GRPO advantage calculation
- Mechanism-aware rewards > auxiliary losses for GRPO integration
- Works with standard LoRA, no architecture changes needed

#### Why This Matters:
Nemotron-3-Nano-30B is a MoE model (128 experts + 1 shared, 6 active per token). If we do GRPO with LoRA, we MUST consider routing balance or risk routing collapse. This could explain why simple GRPO might not work well on this model.

### 3. Tool-N1: Pure RL Outperforms SFT+RL -- COUNTERINTUITIVE FINDING

**Paper**: NVlabs/Tool-N1 (arxiv 2505.00024)
**Key finding**: Pure RL with GRPO outperforms both "Reason-SFT then RL" and "No-Reason SFT then RL"

#### Details:
- Uses GRPO with binary reward (format correctness + functional correctness)
- No reasoning annotations needed -- model learns reasoning implicitly from task success
- Tool-N1-7B outperforms GPT-4o on tool-calling benchmarks
- Training on just 5,518 distilled trajectories achieves strong results

#### Implication:
For our puzzle types (which are all programmatically verifiable), pure GRPO with binary accuracy rewards may be more effective than generating elaborate SFT traces first. The model can learn its own reasoning strategy.

### 5. Advanced GRPO Multi-Reward Training Recipe (HuggingFace Cookbook)

Complete working recipe for GRPO with 4 reward functions:
1. **Exact format compliance** (3.0 points): Full <reasoning>...</reasoning><solution>...</solution> match
2. **Approximate format** (0.5 per tag): Partial credit for structural elements
3. **Answer correctness** (3.0 exact, 1.5 within 10%, 0.5 within 20%, -0.5 wrong)
4. **Number extraction** (1.5): Can parse numerical answer

Config:
- Model: Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct with QLoRA (4-bit)
- LoRA: r=16, alpha=32, target=[q_proj, v_proj], dropout=0.1
- GRPO: LR=5e-6, batch=2, grad_accum=8, max_grad_norm=0.1
- 8 generations per step (default)
- max_prompt_length=1024, max_completion_length=1024

#### Adaptation for Our Competition:
Replace format tags with `<think></think>` and `\boxed{}`. Replace GSM8K reward with puzzle verifiers.

### 10. Bit Manipulation Uses Majority/Choice Operations
From lucian kucera's prompt (0.76 score, 100th place):
- bit_manipulation puzzles include **Majority** and **Choice** functions
- Majority: `lambda a, b, c: (a & b) | (b & c) | (c & a)` with two constant masks
- Choice: `lambda c, t, f: (c & t) | (~c & f)` with two constant masks
- These are NOT simple XOR/NOT/rotation — they require per-bit analysis
- Our current CoT generator doesn't cover Majority/Choice operations
- **Must add these to bit_manipulation CoT for better training data**
